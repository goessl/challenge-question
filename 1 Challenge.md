A set of one dimensional, orthonormal wavefunctions $\{\phi_i:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{C}\}_{i\in\{1,2,\dots,N\}}$,
$$
	\phi_i\in L_\mathbb{R}^2 \qquad \Braket{\phi_i|\phi_j}=\int_\mathbb{R}\phi_i^*(x)\phi_j(x)\,\mathrm{d}x=\delta_{ij} \ ,
$$
that all fulfill the Schrödinger equation for some potential $V:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$
$$
	-\frac{1}{2}\phi_i''+V\phi_i = E_i\phi_i \ ,
$$
have a density (probability density distribution) $n:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}_0^+$
$$
	n = \sum_{i=1}^N|\varphi_i|^2 \ .
$$
What is the density to kinetic energy functional $T[n]$
$$
	T = -\frac{1}{2}\int_\mathbb{R}\sum_{i=1}^N\phi_i^*(x)\phi_i''(x)\,\mathrm{d}x \ ?
$$
