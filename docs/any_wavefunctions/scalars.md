#Scalars

## Question

For a given $c\in\mathbb{R}_0^+$ find any $a, b\in\mathbb{R}$ such that $a^2+b^2=c$.

## Solution

$$
    \begin{aligned}
        a &= \sqrt{c}\cos\alpha \\
        b &= \sqrt{c}\sin\alpha
    \end{aligned} \qquad \alpha\in[0, 2\pi[
$$

## Proof

$$
    \begin{aligned}
        a^2+b^2 &= \left(\sqrt{c}\cos\alpha\right)^2+\left(\sqrt{c}\sin\alpha\right)^2 \\
        &= c\cos^2\alpha + c\sin^2\alpha \\
        &= c\left(\cos^2\alpha+\sin^2\alpha\right) \\
        &= c
    \end{aligned}
$$
